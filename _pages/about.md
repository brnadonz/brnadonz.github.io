I am a third year [MD/PhD](https://mdphd.johnshopkins.edu/) student in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins, advised by Dr. Adam Charles in the [Neural Signal and Computation Lab](https://www.bme.jhu.edu/ascharles/). My current focus is in developing novel methods to enable and interpret fast fluorescent imaging of high-speed neural dynamics. Broadly, I am interested in computational imaging, neuroscience, dynamical systems, and optimization. I hope to apply these tools in advancing our understanding of healthy neural circuits as well as the aberrations that occur as a result of neurologic/psychiatric disorders, with the end-goal of improving diagnosis and treatment.

Previously, I completed a combined BS/MSE in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Michigan. There, I was fortunate to be advised by Dr. Parag Patil, developing computational methods for optimizing DBS for Parkinson's disease. I also spent time  investigating mechanisms of radioresistance in low-grade glioma and developing low-cost, sustained-release drug delivery platforms, in the labs of Dr. Maria Castro (University of Michigan) and Dr. Patrick Smith (Michigan State University, St. Andrews), respectively.

In my free time, I like to run, play soccer/basketball with friends, and cook! I am also learning to play the [dizi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dizi_(instrument)) in preparation for a performance with my family!
